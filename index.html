The One-Way Trip ~ wasn't ~

The Bad Future

The Monadverse fell. Mongrod got the Monorb, corrupted the Great Elder Tree, turned the jungle to ash. Molandak — leader of the Five, the porcupine from landak — lost everyone. Even her.

The One-Way Trip

The Doctor built a time machine from the wreckage. "One-way," he said, sealing the rift behind Molandak. The hedgehog crossed back to the past with a beam gun and a vow. Behind him, in the burning future, stood Molly.

The Bow

She tied the bow before she crossed. Her last act in the bad future. Pink ribbon, knotted with one of his quills she'd kept. Not jewelry — a tether. The thing she'd hold onto when the timeline tried to forget her.

The Witness

She made it through. Different mechanism — the Elder Tree responded to the rift, or the Monorb's residual energy folded space, or she just refused to accept "one-way." Now she's here. Proof Molandak's mission has weight. Proof not everything in the bad future was lost.

Why She Doesn't Fight

She's not here to swing a beam gun. Molandak does that. Chog paints illusions, Salmonad invents, Moyaki cooks, Mouch flies recon. Molly watches the jungle. She knows what it looks like when this place dies. She's making sure it doesn't.

Mongrod Is Coming

He hasn't found the Monorb yet — that's why she still gets to exist. But he's looking. Every buyer of $MOLLY is, in a small way, voting for the timeline where Molandak wins. The jungle remembers who showed up.
